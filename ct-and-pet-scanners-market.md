<p><p>CT (Computed Tomography) and PET (Positron Emission Tomography) scanners are advanced medical imaging technologies used to diagnose various diseases and conditions in the body.</p><p>CT scanners use X-rays and computer algorithms to create detailed cross-sectional images of the body. They are primarily used for detecting bone fractures, lung abnormalities, and cancers. CT scanners have evolved to offer faster scanning times, higher image resolution, and reduced radiation exposure, leading to improved patient care.</p><p>PET scanners, on the other hand, use a radioactive tracer injected into the body to detect cellular activity and metabolism. They are commonly used for diagnosing cancer, heart disease, and brain disorders. PET scanners provide dynamic and functional images, allowing for better disease detection and treatment planning.</p><p>The CT and PET scanners market has witnessed significant growth in recent years, and this trend is expected to continue during the forecast period.
