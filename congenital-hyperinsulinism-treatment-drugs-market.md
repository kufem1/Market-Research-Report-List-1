<p><p>Congenital hyperinsulinism (CHI) refers to a rare genetic disorder that affects the regulation of insulin secretion in infants and children. It is characterized by excessive and uncontrolled insulin release from pancreatic beta cells, leading to severe hypoglycemia (low blood sugar levels). To treat this condition, several drugs are available in the market that aim to manage the abnormal insulin levels.</p><p>One of the primary treatment options for CHI is diazoxide, which is a potassium channel opener. It helps reduce insulin release and increase blood sugar levels. Other drugs used include octreotide, which inhibits insulin release, and glucagon, which helps raise blood sugar levels during hypoglycemic emergencies. In some cases, patients may require surgical intervention to remove part or all of the pancreas to control insulin secretion.</p><p>The Congenital Hyperinsulinism Treatment Drugs Market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period.
