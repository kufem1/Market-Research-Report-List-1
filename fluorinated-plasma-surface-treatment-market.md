<p><p>Fluorinated plasma surface treatment is a technique used to modify the surface properties of materials. It involves the use of fluorine-based plasma to enhance the adhesion, wettability, and other surface characteristics. There are two types of markets in this field: low-pressure plasma and atmospheric plasma. Low-pressure plasma treatment is performed in a vacuum chamber, making it suitable for delicate materials and complex shapes. Atmospheric plasma treatment, on the other hand, can be done at ambient pressure and is more versatile, providing a cost-effective solution for larger production volumes and diverse material types.</p></p>
