<p><p>Robotic-assisted hip replacement systems can be divided into three types: passive systems, semi-active systems, and active systems. Passive systems rely on pre-programmed guidance for the surgeon to follow during the procedure. Semi-active systems allow the surgeon to control certain aspects of the surgery while the robot assists with others. Active systems provide the surgeon with real-time feedback and assistance throughout the procedure, allowing for a more precise and customized approach. Each type offers different levels of automation and control for the surgeon during hip replacement surgery.</p></p>
