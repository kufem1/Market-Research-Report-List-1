<p><p>Intracranial support catheters are medical devices used to provide support and drain fluids in the brain. There are two main types of intracranial support catheters on the market: adjustable and non-adjustable. Adjustable catheters allow for customization of length and position, providing more flexibility during procedures. Non-adjustable catheters have a fixed length and are used for more straightforward applications. Both types play a crucial role in managing intracranial pressure and promoting proper fluid drainage in patients with neurological conditions.</p></p>
