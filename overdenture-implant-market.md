<p><p>Overdenture implant, also known as dental implant-retained overdenture, is a dental prosthesis used to replace missing teeth. It involves the use of dental implants, which are surgically placed into the jawbone to provide stability and support for the overdenture. Unlike conventional dentures that rely on suction or adhesive to stay in place, overdentures snap onto the dental implants, offering increased stability and preventing slippage while speaking or eating.</p><p>The global overdenture implant market is experiencing significant growth due to the rising prevalence of dental disorders, increasing geriatric population, and growing awareness about the benefits of dental implants over traditional dentures. Additionally, advancements in implant technology and techniques have greatly improved the success rates of overdenture implants, driving market growth.</p><p>Moreover, the market is witnessing a surge in demand for cosmetic dentistry, with patients seeking more natural-looking and comfortable teeth replacement options. Overdenture implants offer enhanced aesthetics, improved oral function, and increased patient satisfaction, contributing to their increasing adoption.</p><p>Furthermore, the COVID-19 pandemic has also impacted the market positively. As dental clinics reopened and patients became more conscious about oral health, the demand for dental implants, including overdenture implants, has surged.</p><p>In terms of market trends, the use of digital dentistry and computer-aided design/computer-aided manufacturing (CAD/CAM) technology has revolutionized the implant dentistry field. <p><p>The overdenture implant market consists of two main types: tooth-supported overdentures and implant-supported overdentures. Tooth-supported overdentures are removable dentures that are supported by the remaining natural teeth in the mouth. Implant-supported overdentures, on the other hand, are supported by dental implants that have been surgically placed in the jawbone. These implants provide a stable foundation for the denture and improve its functionality and comfort. Both types of overdentures aim to enhance the patient's quality of life by improving the fit, stability, and retention of the dentures.</p></p>
