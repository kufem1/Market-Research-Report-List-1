<p><p>Ovulation Predictor Kits can be divided into two main types based on the method of testing: urine-based and saliva-based. Urine-based kits work by detecting the surge in luteinizing hormone levels that typically occurs 24-48 hours before ovulation. Saliva-based kits, on the other hand, detect changes in estrogen levels by examining saliva samples under a microscope. Both types of kits are sought after by women looking to track their fertility and pinpoint the best time for conception.</p></p>
