<p><p>Scratch-resistant tiles come in various market types including polishing tile, glazed tile, vitrified tile, and other specialized options. Polishing tiles have a smooth finish achieved through polishing, providing a classy look. Glazed tiles are coated with a layer of liquid glass, making them stain and scratch-resistant. Vitrified tiles are made by combining clay with silica and quartz to create a non-porous surface that resists scratches. Other specialized options may include mosaic tiles, terrazzo tiles, or composite tiles with unique scratch-resistant properties.</p></p>
