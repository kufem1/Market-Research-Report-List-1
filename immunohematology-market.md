<p><p>Immunohematology, also known as blood banking, is a branch of medicine that focuses on the study of blood groups, blood typing, and compatibility testing for blood transfusions. It involves the collection, testing, processing, and storage of blood and its components to ensure safe and efficient blood transfusions. Immunohematology plays a critical role in preventing transfusion reactions and ensuring that patients receive compatible blood products.</p><p>The global immunohematology market is experiencing steady growth and is expected to continue growing at a CAGR of 7.00% during the forecast period. This growth can be attributed to various factors, including an increasing number of surgical procedures, rising demand for blood and blood products, advancements in technology, and a growing elderly population.</p><p>One of the latest trends in the immunohematology market is the adoption of automation and digitization in blood typing and screening processes. This trend has led to increased efficiency, reduced errors, and improved patient outcomes. Automation enables the rapid screening of blood samples, reduces human intervention, and streamlines the blood testing process.</p><p>Another trend in the immunohematology market is the increasing demand for specialized blood products. With advancements in medical treatments and an aging population, the need for specific blood components, such as red blood cells, platelets, and plasma, is growing. This trend has led to the development of blood fractionation technologies and specialized blood products to cater to specific patient needs.</p><p>In conclusion, the immunohematology market is expected to witness steady growth during the forecast period, driven by factors such as increasing surgical procedures, advancements in technology, and the rising demand for specialized blood products. The adoption of automation and digitization in blood typing and screening processes is also a prominent trend in the market.</p></p>
