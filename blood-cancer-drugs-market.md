<p><p>Blood cancer drugs can be broadly classified into two types: blood chemotherapy drugs and blood targeted therapy drugs. Blood chemotherapy drugs are medications that are used to kill cancer cells throughout the body and are typically given through injection or orally. On the other hand, blood targeted therapy drugs are designed to specifically target cancer cells by blocking the signals that allow their growth. These drugs work by interfering with specific molecules involved in the growth and spread of blood cancer cells.</p></p>
