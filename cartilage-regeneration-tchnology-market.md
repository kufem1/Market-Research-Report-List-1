ACI involves the transplantation of the patient's own cartilage cells, which are harvested, cultured, and then implanted to repair damaged cartilage. This technique offers the advantage of using the patient's own cells, reducing the risk of rejection or disease transmission. Other markets within cartilage regeneration technology may include techniques such as microfracture, osteochondral transplant, and cell-based implants. These approaches aim to stimulate cartilage regrowth or replace damaged tissue through different methods.</p></p>
