<p><p>A prosthetic heart valve, also known as an artificial heart valve, is a medical device implanted in patients with diseased heart valves to either replace the damaged valve or repair its functionality. The main function of a prosthetic heart valve is to regulate the blood flow and maintain the proper functioning of the heart.</p><p>The prosthetic heart valve market has shown significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue growing at a Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) of 11.2% during the forecast period. The increasing prevalence of cardiovascular diseases and the rising geriatric population are major factors driving market growth. Additionally, technological advancements in heart valve replacement and repair procedures, such as minimally invasive surgeries, have further propelled market expansion.</p><p>Moreover, the adoption of transcatheter heart valve replacement (THVR) procedures has gained prominence due to their minimally invasive nature and fast recovery time. THVR offers benefits such as reduced hospital stay and lower procedural risks, which have increased its demand among patients and physicians.</p><p>In terms of trends, the market is witnessing a shift towards the use of tissue-based heart valves over mechanical valves. Tissue valves, made from animal tissues or human tissue grafts, offer improved long-term durability and reduce the need for lifelong anticoagulation therapy compared to mechanical valves.</p><p>Geographically, North America dominates the prosthetic heart valve market due to the high prevalence of cardiovascular diseases and the presence of well-established healthcare infrastructure. However, Asia Pacific is anticipated to witness significant growth in the coming years, attributed to the growing healthcare expenditure, improving healthcare infrastructure, and rising awareness about heart valve diseases.</p><p>In conclusion, the prosthetic heart valve and artificial heart valve market is experiencing robust growth with technological advancements and increasing adoption of minimally invasive procedures. <p><p>The prosthetic heart valve market is divided into three main types: transcatheter heart valve, tissue heart valve, and mechanical heart valve. Transcatheter heart valves are inserted through a catheter, without the need for open-heart surgery. Tissue heart valves are made from animal tissue, which is then treated and shaped into a valve. Mechanical heart valves, on the other hand, are made from durable materials such as titanium and carbon, requiring lifelong anticoagulation therapy. Each type has its pros and cons, and the choice depends on the patient's condition, age, and lifestyle.</p></p>
