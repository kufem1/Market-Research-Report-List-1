<p><p>Enzymes for baking come in various types tailored for specific functions. Amylase enzymes assist in breaking down starches into sugars, while protease enzymes help in the breakdown of proteins for dough development. Cellulase enzymes aid in enhancing dough texture, while oxidase enzymes improve dough strength and volume. Other enzymes may include lipase for fat breakdown, xylanase for better crumb structure, and glucose oxidase for dough stability. Each enzyme plays a unique role in improving the quality and characteristics of baked goods.</p></p>
