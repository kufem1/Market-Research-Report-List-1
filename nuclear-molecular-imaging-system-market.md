<p><strong>The Nuclear Molecular Imaging System Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Positron Emission Tomography</li><li>Single-photon Emission Computed Tomography</li><li>Planar Scintigraphy Systems</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Nuclear molecular imaging systems are used for diagnostic purposes to visualize and analyze the function of organs, tissues, and cells. The market for these systems can be categorized into three main types: Positron Emission Tomography (PET), Single-photon Emission Computed Tomography (SPECT), and Planar Scintigraphy Systems. PET utilizes radiotracers to detect gamma rays, while SPECT utilizes radioactive isotopes to produce 3D images. Planar scintigraphy systems create 2D images of the distribution of radioactive substances in the body. Each type of system offers unique benefits for different applications in medical imaging.</p></p>
