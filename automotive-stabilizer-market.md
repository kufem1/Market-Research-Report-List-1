<p><p>Automotive stabilizer bars are key components in a vehicle's suspension system that help reduce body roll and improve stability during cornering. There are two main types of stabilizer bars: solid and hollow. </p><p>Solid stabilizer bars are made from a solid piece of metal, usually steel, and are known for their durability and ability to effectively reduce body roll. </p><p>Hollow stabilizer bars, on the other hand, have a hollow core, which makes them lighter and more flexible than solid bars. They are often used in high-performance vehicles to provide a balance between stiffness and weight savings.</p></p>
