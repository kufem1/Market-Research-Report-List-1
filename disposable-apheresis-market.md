<p><ul><li>Centrifugation</li><li>Membrane Separation</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The disposable apheresis market includes two main types: centrifugation and membrane separation. Centrifugation is a process where blood components are separated based on density by spinning at high speeds in a centrifuge. Membrane separation uses semi-permeable membranes to selectively filter blood components based on size and charge. Both methods are commonly used in medical treatments such as plasma exchange, red blood cell exchange, and stem cell collection. The market for disposable apheresis devices continues to grow due to increasing demand for these procedures in healthcare settings.</p></p>
