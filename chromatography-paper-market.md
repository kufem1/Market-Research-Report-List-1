<p><p>Chromatography paper is classified into three types based on thickness: under 0.25mm, 0.25mm-0.4mm, and over 0.4mm. Under 0.25mm paper is typically used for high-resolution separation of compounds, while 0.25mm-0.4mm paper is suitable for general purpose chromatography applications. Over 0.4mm paper is preferred for preparative chromatography, allowing for higher sample loading capacity. Each type of chromatography paper offers distinct advantages depending on the specific requirements of the separation process, making it essential for researchers to choose the right type for their experiments.</p></p>
