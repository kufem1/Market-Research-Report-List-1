<p><p>Knife valves and gate valves are two types of valves commonly used in industrial applications to control the flow of fluid through pipes. </p><p>Knife valves, also known as slide valves or gate valves, consist of a sharp-edged disc or blade that slides into a seat to regulate the flow of fluid. These valves are designed for on/off applications and are typically used in systems where low pressure drop and tight shutoff are required. Knife valves are commonly used in industries such as water treatment, chemical processing, and power generation.</p><p>Gate valves, on the other hand, have a gate-like disc that moves up and down to control the flow of fluid. These valves provide a straight-through flow path when fully open and have low pressure drop. Gate valves are often used in applications where a full-bore flow is required, such as in the oil and gas industry, water distribution systems, and wastewater treatment plants.</p><p>The global knife valves and gate valves market is expected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 6.5% during the forecast period.
