<p><p>Legionnaire disease testing market consists of various types. Urinary antigen testing detects a specific Legionella bacterium antigen in urine samples. Direct Fluorescent Antibody (DFA) Stain involves staining Legionella bacteria with fluorescent tags for identification under a microscope. Combination testing uses both culture and urinary antigen testing methods to confirm the presence of Legionella bacteria. Other types may include PCR-based tests and serology. These testing methods help diagnose Legionnaire disease by detecting the bacteria or its antibodies in different samples, facilitating accurate and efficient identification of the disease.</p></p>
